# Variant 3: LinkedIn Manufacturing Process Post

**Framework:** 4P (Promise, Picture, Proof, Push)
**Platform:** LinkedIn organic post
**Use:** Week 3 platform presence post. Three to four short paragraphs. No graphic required. End with a question or a concrete observation.
**Character budget:** "See more" at ~210 chars on mobile. Hook must land before that.

---

## Copy

**[P1 — Promise. Hook before "see more", 176 chars]**

> Hotel opening dates do not move. When a principal hands us a programme, every piece of joinery, furniture, and cabinetry is already scheduled across our own workshops. The date on the contract is the date we plan to.

**[P2 — Picture. What that looks like in practice.]**

> Most fit-out projects run across multiple trade contractors, each with their own suppliers and their own lead times. A delay in one propagates to all of them. Our model is different: joinery, furniture production, door manufacturing, and surface finishing are all in-house. We hold the critical path ourselves.

**[P3 — Proof. Verifiable.]**

> We have been doing this since 2004, across hotel and hospitality projects in the GCC, the UK, and Europe. In twenty years, the projects that have come back to us a second time are almost always the ones where the opening went smoothly.

**[P4 — Push. Question or concrete observation.]**

> If you are planning a hotel fit-out and the opening date is already fixed, that is the single most useful thing to tell the contractor early. What does your programme look like?

## Visual brief

This variant does not require an image. The brief specifies "no graphic needed." If one is added later:

**Asset:** 1080x1080 (1:1, LinkedIn primary)
**Style:** Workshop interior. A length of veneer or a door panel being finished. Near-monochrome. Industrial-but-precise: fluorescent workshop light, clean surfaces, visible craft. No people visible in the frame; hands on material only if present.
**Mood:** Quiet craft. Evidence of process, not aspiration.
**Forbidden:** Staged lifestyle, render imagery, over-saturated finishes, any competitor brand visible.
