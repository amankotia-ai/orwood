# Variant 5: Personal Email — Past Client Re-engagement

**Framework:** BAB (Before, After, Bridge) — light-handed. The before/after is implicit: a project that nearly missed its date vs. one that did not. The bridge is ORWOOD's model.
**Platform:** Personal email. Plain text only. Personal addresses only. No bulk send, no newsletter template.
**Use:** Week 4 relationship and pipeline. Maximum 150 words. Personalise [bracketed] fields per recipient.
**Tone:** Personal, direct, plain. This is a note from a person, not a company.

---

## Subject line options (plain, no hype)

- A project update from ORWOOD
- An update, and a question
- [City] project wrapped — and one capability worth knowing

---

## Email Body (~125 words)

> [Name],
>
> We wrapped a hotel fit-out in [city] recently. The scope ran from initial design through joinery and custom furniture, all produced in our own workshops and delivered to the opening date.
>
> One thing worth knowing if it is useful: ORWOOD Doors covers custom door sets for hospitality: fire-rated and acoustic options, designed and manufactured in-house. Easier to specify than sourcing separately.
>
> If you have anything coming up where a single team covering design, manufacture, and delivery would help, I would be glad to hear about it. No agenda beyond that.
>
> [Sender name]
> ORWOOD
> orwood.com/contact

---

## Personalisation checklist

- [ ] Replace [Name] with the recipient's first name
- [ ] Replace [city] with an actual ORWOOD project city relevant to or near the recipient
- [ ] Replace [Sender name] with the actual name of the person sending (not "The ORWOOD Team")
- [ ] Confirm the recipient is a past client or a warm contact (not a cold name)
- [ ] Send from a personal email address, not a company alias
- [ ] Log sends in a simple tracker to confirm 10-20 sent and to follow up any replies
