# Variant 2: LinkedIn Specifier Connection Note

**Framework:** PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution) — soft register. The problem is implied (finding a fit-out partner who executes design intent), not stated. No hard sell.
**Platform:** LinkedIn connection request note (max 300 chars) or short follow-up DM
**Use:** Week 2 specifier outreach. Personalise the [bracketed] fields per contact before sending. One note per contact; no bulk send.

---

## Connection Note Copy (300-char limit)

> [Name], we completed a hotel fit-out in [city] covering design, joinery, and custom FF&E from our own workshops. I came across your work on [project name]. Thought there might be an overlap worth a short conversation.

**Character count:** 216 chars. Pass (max 300).

---

## Follow-up DM Copy (if connection accepted, no response after 5-7 days)

> [Name], thanks for connecting.
>
> If you have a hospitality project coming up where you need a fit-out team that can hold the programme from design through delivery, we are worth a conversation. We manufacture the joinery and furniture in-house, which tends to matter when the opening date is fixed.
>
> No pressure either way.

**Character count:** ~295 chars across three short paragraphs. Readable on mobile.

---

## Search targeting guidance (LinkedIn)

Search terms to build the outreach list:
- "interior architect" + "hospitality" + Dubai
- "interior designer" + "hotel" + Riyadh
- "interior architect" + "hospitality" + London

Filter: 2nd-degree connections first (warmer path). Check for shared connections and name-drop where genuine.

---

## Personalisation checklist (per contact)

- [ ] Replace [Name] with first name
- [ ] Replace [city] with actual project city (use a real one from ORWOOD's delivered work)
- [ ] Replace [project name] with a specific project visible on their LinkedIn or firm site
- [ ] Confirm the contact is active in hospitality (not residential-only)
- [ ] If SILADU has a material sheet at time of outreach, attach as supporting proof (specifiers respond to material proof — per ICP context)
