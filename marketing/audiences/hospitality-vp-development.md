# VP Development at a GCC hospitality group

**Segment:** Hospitality principals (hotel owners, hospitality groups, developers)
**Status:** draft
**Weight:** 9

## Role & firmographics

- VP or Director of Development at a regional hospitality group (10–40 properties)
- Based in Dubai, Riyadh, or Doha; portfolio expanding across GCC
- Reports to CEO/board; owns the pipeline from land acquisition to pre-opening
- Team of 3–8 project managers; relies on external contractors for fit-out

## Jobs to be done

1. Deliver hotel interiors turnkey to a fixed pre-opening date — delays cost licence fees and lost revenue
2. De-risk multi-trade fit-out with one accountable contract so the board sees a single line item
3. Maintain brand-standard consistency across properties in different countries
4. Reduce procurement complexity — one partner for joinery, FF&E, doors, and installation

## Pains

- Coordinating 6+ subcontractors across joinery, furniture, doors, MEP interfaces — scope gaps cause delays
- Shipping and customs in GCC add lead-time uncertainty
- Past contractors delivered show-flat quality that degraded within 18 months of operation
- Board scrutiny on cost overruns; needs fixed-price confidence before signing

## Objections

- "Can you handle the scale? We need 200+ rooms delivered simultaneously."
- "We've been burned by contractors who subcontract everything — do you actually manufacture?"
- "Our brand has strict FF&E specs. Can you match operator standards exactly?"

## Buying triggers

- New hotel/resort in the pipeline with a confirmed opening date
- Existing contractor relationship failed on the last project (delays, defects)
- Brand operator (Marriott, Accor, IHG) requires pre-qualified fit-out partners

## Vocabulary

- Pre-opening schedule, key date, practical completion, snag list
- FF&E spec, OS&E, brand standards, mock-up room
- Turnkey, single-point accountability, EPC-style contract
- Value engineering (not cost-cutting — maintaining design intent at lower cost)

## Content that resonates

- Hospitality case studies with timeline data (bid → handover)
- Proof of owned manufacturing (factory tour, CNC capability)
- References from brand operators or hotel owners in GCC
- "One team, one number" messaging — single accountability
