# UHNW homeowner commissioning a bespoke villa interior

**Segment:** Luxury residential clients
**Status:** draft
**Weight:** 5

## Role & firmographics

- Ultra-high-net-worth individual or family office representative
- Commissioning interior fit-out for a primary residence, villa, or penthouse (typically 300–1,500 sqm)
- Based in GCC or Türkiye; may have properties in multiple countries
- Works through an interior designer or architect; may also engage directly
- Decision-making is personal and emotional — this is their home, not an asset

## Jobs to be done

1. Bespoke joinery, kitchens, wardrobes, and libraries finished like furniture — heirloom quality
2. A single trusted partner who coordinates all interior trades so the homeowner doesn't manage contractors
3. Materials and finishes that feel exclusive — not available off-the-shelf
4. Delivery to a move-in date without compromising on quality

## Pains

- Past experiences with contractors who promised bespoke but delivered from a catalogue
- Coordinating kitchen, wardrobes, doors, and panelling across different suppliers
- Privacy and discretion — doesn't want their home on a contractor's Instagram
- Late-stage design changes are inevitable; needs a partner who accommodates without hostility

## Objections

- "I want to see examples of residential work at this level — not hotels."
- "Will my project get the same attention as your large commercial contracts?"
- "I need absolute discretion. Will my home appear in your marketing?"

## Buying triggers

- New villa or penthouse build reaching interior fit-out stage
- Interior designer recommending a joinery/fit-out partner
- Dissatisfaction with current contractor's quality on an ongoing project

## Vocabulary

- Bespoke, custom, one-of-a-kind
- Walk-in wardrobe, dressing room, library, wine room
- Heirloom quality, hand-finished, artisan
- Move-in date, snagging, final inspection

## Content that resonates

- Residential case studies (with discretion — anonymised if needed)
- Close-up photography of joinery details, finishes, and material quality
- Joinery & Manufacturing + Furniture Solutions service pages
- Proof of in-house capability — "made, not just specified"
