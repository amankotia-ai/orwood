# Principal at a hospitality-focused architecture studio

**Segment:** Specifiers — architects & interior designers
**Status:** draft
**Weight:** 8

## Role & firmographics

- Founding principal or design director at a 15–50 person architecture practice
- Specialises in hospitality: hotels, resorts, F&B, wellness
- Based in London, Istanbul, or Dubai; projects span GCC, Türkiye, and Europe
- Specifies materials and contractors; recommends (but doesn't contract) fit-out partners to owners

## Jobs to be done

1. Find a contractor who executes design intent to furniture-grade tolerance — not "close enough"
2. Source certified acoustic/fire-rated door sets that integrate with the design language
3. Have a manufacturing partner who can prototype and iterate during design development
4. Protect their reputation — the finished interior reflects on the architect, not just the contractor

## Pains

- Contractors who value-engineer away design intent without consulting the architect
- Joinery tolerances that look fine in the factory but fail at site junctions
- Door manufacturers whose standard profiles don't suit the project's aesthetic
- Lack of early contractor engagement — brought in too late to influence buildability

## Objections

- "We specify; we don't want the contractor influencing the design direction."
- "Can your factory produce a 1:1 mock-up panel before we commit?"
- "We've had contractors claim 'furniture-grade' who delivered commercial-grade. Show us."

## Buying triggers

- New hospitality project entering design development (RIBA Stage 3–4)
- Client asking the architect to recommend a fit-out contractor
- Previous contractor relationship ended badly — seeking a replacement

## Vocabulary

- Design intent, detail drawing, shop drawing review
- Furniture-grade tolerance, shadow gap, reveal detail
- Mock-up panel, prototype, material sample
- RIBA stages, tender package, novation

## Content that resonates

- Case study galleries showing design-to-reality fidelity (architect credit visible)
- Manufacturing page — CNC capability, quality control, material expertise
- Joinery & Manufacturing service page
- Evidence of architect collaboration (not just contractor execution)
