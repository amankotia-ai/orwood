# Senior interior designer at a luxury ID practice

**Segment:** Specifiers — architects & interior designers
**Status:** draft
**Weight:** 6

## Role & firmographics

- Senior designer or associate at a 5–20 person interior design studio
- Focus on luxury hospitality and high-end residential — suites, villas, penthouses, F&B
- Based in London, Milan, or Istanbul; projects in GCC and Europe
- Works closely with clients on material selection; needs contractors who can realise custom finishes

## Jobs to be done

1. Translate material boards and finish schedules into real surfaces — veneer matching, custom lacquers, metalwork integration
2. Find a joinery partner who treats each piece as furniture, not cabinetry
3. Maintain creative control through the manufacturing process — review shop drawings, approve samples
4. Deliver on the client's emotional expectation — "it has to feel like this"

## Pains

- Contractors who substitute materials without consulting the designer
- Veneer grain-matching and finish consistency across large runs
- Long lead times on custom pieces jeopardise project schedules
- Residential clients change their minds — needs a manufacturer who can adapt late

## Objections

- "I need to visit the factory and see your finishing capabilities in person."
- "Can you match this specific veneer/stone/metal finish I've specified?"
- "Your website shows large-scale hospitality. Do you do detailed bespoke residential work?"

## Buying triggers

- Luxury project entering FF&E specification phase
- Client requesting bespoke joinery (library, dressing room, kitchen) beyond standard millwork
- Current joinery supplier can't meet the required finish quality

## Vocabulary

- Finish schedule, veneer book-match, quarter-sawn
- Bespoke joinery, millwork, cabinetry
- FF&E specification, COM (customer's own material)
- Shop drawing, sample approval, site measure

## Content that resonates

- Residential case studies with close-up joinery/finish photography
- SILADU materials & surfaces capability (veneer and surface development)

- Evidence of designer-contractor collaboration and sample approval process
