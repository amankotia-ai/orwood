# Head of Real Estate at a corporate developer delivering Class-A offices

**Segment:** Commercial property owners & developers
**Status:** draft
**Weight:** 7

## Role & firmographics

- Head of Real Estate or Development Director at a commercial developer (5–20 active projects)
- Delivering Grade-A office towers, corporate HQs, and mixed-use lobbies
- Based in GCC, Türkiye, or London; often developing for institutional tenants or owner-occupiers
- Manages relationships with 3–5 fit-out contractors on a preferred-supplier panel

## Jobs to be done

1. Fit out HQs, lobbies, and workplaces to a single move-in date — tenant lease commencement is non-negotiable
2. Deliver brand-carrying interiors that survive daily commercial use (10+ year lifecycle)
3. Consolidate fit-out scope (joinery, furniture, doors, finishes) under fewer contracts to reduce coordination risk
4. Meet sustainability and fire-safety certifications for institutional tenants

## Pains

- Tenant fit-out timelines are compressed — often 12–16 weeks from lease signing to move-in
- Multiple subcontractors = finger-pointing when defects appear post-handover
- Lobby and reception areas are brand statements — generic solutions damage asset value
- Fire-rated doors and acoustic partitions are a recurring certification headache

## Objections

- "We need certified fire-rated door sets with test reports — can you supply and install?"
- "Our tenants require WELL/LEED-adjacent material specs. Can you source compliant finishes?"
- "We prefer local contractors for warranty and defect-liability. Where is your nearest team?"

## Buying triggers

- New commercial tower reaching fit-out stage
- Existing preferred contractor failing on quality or timeline
- Institutional tenant with specific fit-out requirements (bank, tech HQ, embassy)

## Vocabulary

- Practical completion, defect-liability period, retention
- Cat A / Cat B fit-out, landlord spec, tenant improvement
- Fire rating, acoustic performance, STC rating
- Preferred supplier list, framework agreement
